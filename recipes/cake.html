Cake

Description

Indulge in the rich, decadent flavors of a perfectly moist chocolate cake that’s sure to satisfy any sweet tooth. Each bite reveals a deep, velvety texture with an intense chocolate taste that lingers long after the last crumb. The cake’s tender crumb pairs beautifully with a luscious chocolate ganache or a simple dusting of powdered sugar, making it versatile enough for both special occasions and everyday treats.

This chocolate cake is a breeze to whip up, with a straightforward recipe that yields consistently delicious results. The ingredients come together to create a smooth, pourable batter that bakes into a dense yet fluffy cake. Whether served warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or enjoyed on its own, this cake is a timeless dessert that promises to be a crowd-pleaser every time.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups flour
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 3/4 cup yoghurt
  • 2 cups white granulated sugar
  • 1 1/4 tbsp vanilla essence/extract
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 2/3 unflavoured vegetable oil
  • 1 1/2 cups milk
  • 2 tsps instant coffee powder
  • 3/4 cup cocoa powder

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 150 °C (300°F) with the fan on and grease and/or line two 8 inch cake tins.
  2. Sift together flour, baking powder and salt. Mix together using a whisk and set aside.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, combine yoghurt, sugar, vanilla and baking soda. Mix until well combined and set aside.
  4. In a microwave safe bowl or jug, combine butter, oil, milk and instant coffee powder. Heat in the microwave (you can do this over a stovetop if you prefer), until the butter is fully melted and the mixture is hot to the touch.
  5. Once hot, add in cocoa powder and stir until dissolved. It's okay if there are a few little lumps.
  6. By this point your yoghurt/baking soda mixture should be slightly frothy. To it add in half of the heated milk mixture and using a whisk mix until combined. Then add in half of your pre-sifted dry ingredients and gently mix with the whisk until just combined. Then add in the remaining milk mixture and gently whisk until just combined. Finish by adding the remaining dry ingredients and gently whisk until just combined. Do not overmix.
  7. Distribute the batter evenly into the two 8 inch cake tins, and then give the cake tins a light bang on the counter to remove any large air bubbles.
  8. Bake for 45 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean. Let the cakes cool in the cake tins for about 20 minutes, before turning them out onto a wire rack to completely cool. Because these cakes have a slight dome, don't let them cool upside down as the layers may break while they're still warm.
